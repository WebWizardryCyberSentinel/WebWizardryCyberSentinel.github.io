            To yonder world, where shadows dance and woes abound,
            Where wretched souls trudge 'neath burdens so profound.
            'Tis not the realm of mirth, nor gentle repose,
            But a realm where woe and folly interpose.
            
            Hark! 'Tis a place where hope, a fleeting bird, doth fly,
            And man, in his folly, doth chase it 'cross the sky.
            For what be this world, if not a pit of despair,
            Where men and women toil, yet find no solace fair?
            
            O, foul world, where darkness doth cloak the light,
            And all that's bright and fair is shrouded in night.
            Where dreams, like wraiths, doth fade 'fore dawn's embrace,
            And life's sweet melodies doth yield to bitter grace.
            
            In this accursed orb, where treachery doth reign,
            And trust is but a whisper, an illusion feigned.
            Where love, a tender flower, oft trampled 'neath hate's tread,
            And virtue, like a vapour, from this world hath fled.
            
            What mockery be this life, where pain is the norm,
            And every glimmer of joy is but a passing storm?
            Where folly doth hold court, and wisdom's voice is lost,
            And the tempest of existence doth exact its cost.
            
            Beneath the firmament, where stars do weep and mourn,
            Lies a world in disarray, where hearts are tattered and torn.
            No respite can be found, no sanctuary for the soul,
            But only an endless tumult, an unceasing toll.
            
            The jest of fate, to birth us into this realm,
            Where every step taken is an unyielding helm.
            No rest for the weary, no balm for the bruised heart,
            But a ceaseless cycle of agony, a world torn apart.
            
            Oh, cursed earth, where deeds of darkness thrive,
            And the noblest of intentions are but shadows alive.
            Where the weight of existence crushes the spirit's flight,
            And the promise of tomorrow is lost in the night.
            
            What tragedy be this stage, where all men must play their part,
            And each act, a testament to the anguish in every heart.
            Where the script is writ in sorrow, and the plot in despair,
            And the audience, none but demons, with a malevolent glare.
            
            A realm of thorns and thistles, where roses dare not grow,
            And the sweet scent of innocence is but a distant echo.
            Where laughter, a rare gem, is soon eclipsed by tears,
            And the measure of a life is marked by unending fears.
            
            Hearken, my fellow travelers, to this tale of woe,
            For in this hapless world, no respite doth it bestow.
            But carry on, we must, through the tempest's wrath,
            And pray for a glimmer of hope in this desolate path.
            
            Yet amidst this grim narrative, a sardonic wit doth play,
            A wry observer, casting truth in a mocking display.
            For in the tapestry of despair, a thread of mirth is sewn,
            And amidst the darkness, a glint of light is known.
